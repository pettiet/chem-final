EN diff → bond

  • < 0.4 → nonpolar covalent
  • 0.4–1.7 → polar covalent
  • ≥ 1.7 → ionic
  • HF = polar covalent (exception!)
metal+nonmetal → ionic · nm+nm → covalent

VSEPR shapes

2 grps, 0 lplinear · 180°
3 grps, 0 lptrig planar · 120°
4 grps, 0 lptetrahedral · 109.5°
4 grps, 1 lptrig pyramidal · ~107°
4 grps, 2 lpbent · ~104.5°
Symmetric shape + same atoms → nonpolar molecule. Asymmetric or lone pair → polar.

IMFs (weak → strong)

  • London dispersion — in EVERY molecule. Bigger = stronger.
  • Dipole–dipole — polar molecules only.
  • H-bonding — only when H is bonded to N, O, or F.
BH₃ & HCl have NO H-bonding.

Nuclear decay

α (⁴₂He)A −4, Z −2
β⁻ (⁰₋₁e)A same, Z +1
β⁺ positronA same, Z −1
γ gammanothing — pure energy
e⁻ captureA same, Z −1
Fission = big → small (power plants). Fusion = small → big (sun).

-ate has more O than -ite.

Naming

  • Ionic: NO prefixes
  • Covalent: USE prefixes
    mono/di/tri/tetra/penta/hexa/hepta/octa/nona/deca
  • Transition metal → Roman numeral (Fe³⁺ = iron(III))
  • Criss-cross charges → formula

5 Reaction types

  • Synthesis: A + B → AB
  • Decomp: AB → A + B
  • Single rep: A + BC → AC + B
  • Double rep: AB + CD → AD + CB
  • Combustion: CxHy + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O
Diatomic: BrINClHOF

Solubility

  • Soluble: NO₃⁻, group 1, NH₄⁺
  • Insoluble: most CO₃²⁻, PO₄³⁻, S²⁻
  • Net ionic = drop spectators (≠ skeleton)

Stoichiometry

  • g → mol → mol → g
  • Mole ratio from coefficients
  • Limiting = smallest (mol ÷ coeff)
  • Never change subscripts

Thermochemistry

  • Exo: ΔH<0 · hot · products lower E
  • Endo: ΔH>0 · cold · products higher E
  • Heating curve FLAT during phase change
  • Heat = total E; Temp = avg KE

⚠ Trap reminders

  • HF = polar covalent (not ionic)
  • BH₃ & HCl: no H-bonding
  • CaCl₂ = calcium chloride (no "di-")
  • Li₂SO₃ = sulfite (3 O)
  • V water ADDED = V₂ − V₁
